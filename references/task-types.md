# Task Types

Use this file when the research task needs a domain-specific question set.

Do not treat all research tasks the same. First classify the task, then shape the subquestions, source priorities, and final report around that type.

## 1. Market or industry research

Core questions:

- What market is being studied?
- How is it segmented?
- How large is it and how fast is it growing?
- What are the main demand drivers?
- What are the biggest risks or uncertainties?

Prioritize:

- official datasets
- regulator or institutional reports
- company filings or investor materials
- credible industry analysis

## 2. Company or startup analysis

Core questions:

- What does the company actually do?
- Who is the target customer?
- How is it positioned?
- What signals show traction, momentum, or weakness?
- What risks stand out?

Prioritize:

- official website
- product docs
- pricing pages
- blog, changelog, launch posts
- jobs/careers pages
- credible press and interviews

## 3. Product or competitor analysis

Core questions:

- What jobs is the product trying to serve?
- Who is the intended user or buyer?
- What is the core value proposition?
- How is it priced and packaged?
- Where does it appear strong or vulnerable?

Prioritize:

- official website and product pages
- pricing pages
- docs and onboarding flow
- release notes or changelog
- comparison pages with caution
- customer stories and review sites as supplemental evidence

## 4. Technical feasibility research

Core questions:

- What exactly is being attempted?
- What approaches are available?
- What constraints matter most?
- What evidence exists that this works in practice?
- What validation would matter next?

Prioritize:

- official docs
- technical papers
- engineering posts
- reputable benchmarks
- repositories and issue trackers

## 5. Regulatory or policy scan

Core questions:

- What jurisdiction matters?
- Is the rule enacted, proposed, interpreted, or enforced?
- What obligations or restrictions matter?
- What remains ambiguous?

Prioritize:

- regulator publications
- statutory or rule text
- official guidance
- credible legal analysis

## 6. Buy / build / partner decision

Core questions:

- What capability is actually needed?
- What constraints matter most: speed, cost, control, quality, compliance?
- What hidden costs exist for each option?
- What would make one option clearly superior?

Prioritize:

- user requirements
- vendor docs and pricing
- implementation case studies
- engineering and integration docs
- security/compliance docs

## 7. Vendor or tool selection

Core questions:

- What requirements are mandatory vs optional?
- Which vendors are realistic candidates?
- How do they differ in capability, pricing, reliability, and integration?
- What trust or lock-in risks matter?

Prioritize:

- official vendor docs
- pricing and plan details
- security/compliance docs
- integration docs
- uptime/status history
- independent reviews with caution

## Cross-type rules

Always identify:

- the real user objective
- the task type
- the decision relevance of each subquestion
- the most likely research failure modes

Always separate:

- confirmed facts
- likely inference
- open uncertainty
