# Claim Matrix

Use this file when the research has multiple important conclusions, conflicting evidence, parallel tracks, or any recommendation that depends on more than one major claim.

Do not organize important research only around sources. Organize it around claims.

## Core rule

For each important conclusion, create an explicit claim record.

Each claim record should answer:

- What is the claim?
- Why does it matter?
- What evidence supports it?
- What evidence weakens or limits it?
- How strong is the evidence?
- What remains unresolved?
- How confident should the final report be?

## Recommended claim record

For each important claim, capture:

1. Claim
2. Why it matters
3. Claim type
4. Support
5. Counter-evidence
6. Scope conditions
7. Freshness
8. Source quality summary
9. Unresolved gaps
10. Confidence
11. Bottom-line status

## Claim types

Use one of:

- confirmed fact
- interpreted fact pattern
- comparative judgment
- causal explanation
- forward-looking inference
- recommendation-supporting judgment

## Quality rules

A good claim should be:

- decision-relevant
- specific
- testable
- scoped
- evidence-linked

For listed-company monopoly / moat / scarcity screening tasks, also capture:

- exact claim wording
- source class
- year / scope / market definition
- whether wording downgrade is needed
- the strongest wording allowed in final delivery

Do not let ranking backbone claims depend on vague market-share figures, undefined time windows, or secondary-only `unique` / `absolute` / `irreplaceable` language.

## Contradiction handling

When support and counter-evidence conflict, compare:

- source quality
- independence
- directness
- recency
- context match

Possible outcomes:

- supported
- tentatively supported
- mixed
- weakened
- unresolved
- contradicted

## Synthesis discipline

Before writing the final report:

1. Identify the 3-7 most important claims.
2. Confirm each has support, counter-evidence, scope, confidence, and status.
3. Identify which claims are load-bearing for the recommendation.
4. Make the weakest load-bearing claim visible in the uncertainty section.
