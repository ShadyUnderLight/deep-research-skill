# Market Entry Memo Example

## Example task

Decide whether to enter a market, where to enter first, and how entry should be sequenced relative to alternatives.

## Expected route

Primary route:

- market entry / regional expansion

## Expected secondary disciplines

Typical supporting disciplines include:

- current-state verification
- source traceability
- quantitative role labeling
- counter-evidence

## Expected visible artifact contract

A strong market-entry memo should make the following visible:

- go / not now / pilot only / phased-entry judgment
- priority relative to alternatives
- hard gates
- first beachhead
- regional hub vs later expansion market
- sequencing logic
- what could change the recommendation

## Typical failure signs

Common failures include:

- turning into a market overview
- presenting country notes without decision architecture
- giving recommendation without hard gates
- using vague sequencing
- relying on precise-looking numbers with unclear roles
- omitting reversal conditions

## Minimal output shape

A compact market-entry memo often includes:

- executive recommendation
- why now / why not now
- hard gates
- country shortlist
- recommended sequencing
- what could change the decision
- risks and uncertainty
- sources
