# Selection Memo Example

## Example task

Choose among defined options and explain why one wins, why another remains credible, and what could change the ranking.

## Expected route

Primary route:

- constrained choice / shortlist

## Expected secondary disciplines

Typical supporting disciplines include:

- source traceability
- quantitative role labeling
- counter-evidence
- current-state verification when the task is time-sensitive

## Expected visible artifact contract

A strong selection memo should make the following visible:

- the real decision question
- the comparison unit
- the shortlist logic
- why the top option wins
- why the runner-up does not win now
- what could change the ranking
- uncertainty that can materially affect the choice

## Typical failure signs

Common failures include:

- turning into a general overview of all options
- listing options without a visible comparison unit
- presenting uncertainty only as a generic caveat
- showing scores without clarifying their role
- giving a recommendation without reversal logic

## Minimal output shape

A compact selection memo often includes:

- executive summary
- decision architecture
- comparison unit
- top choice and why
- runner-up and why not now
- what would change the ranking
- uncertainty and risks
- sources
